# Evidence Pack

Use this checklist to decide whether an Investor Panel view can be high confidence. Do not require every item for a quick screen; do require enough evidence to avoid inventing facts.

## Minimum Evidence Packet

For a single company:

- Company name, ticker if public, exchange, reporting currency, fiscal year.
- Business model in one paragraph: how it makes money, customers, pricing, distribution.
- Segment or product revenue mix and growth.
- Current or latest available market cap, enterprise value, share count, and price date.
- Income statement trend: revenue, gross margin, operating margin, net income, EPS.
- Cash flow trend: operating cash flow, capex, free cash flow, stock-based compensation.
- Balance sheet: cash, debt, maturities, liquidity, working capital.
- Return profile: ROIC/ROE/ROA where available.
- Valuation: P/E, EV/EBITDA, EV/sales, FCF yield, or DCF assumptions.
- Management and ownership: CEO/CFO, founder status, insider ownership, incentive structure, buybacks/dividends.
- Competitive landscape: key competitors, moat, customer concentration, substitutes.
- Industry and macro context: growth drivers, cyclicality, rates, regulation, commodity or FX exposure.
- Recent evidence: latest annual/interim/quarterly report, earnings call, investor presentation, material announcements, news.
- Risks: leverage, dilution, regulation, technology disruption, governance, customer/supplier concentration, accounting quality.

## Theme / Supply-Chain Evidence

For a theme scan:

- Market and geography: US, HK, A-share, global, or other.
- Time window: current, 3-12 months, cycle view, or user-specified.
- Demand driver: what changed technically, economically, or politically.
- Value-chain map: downstream demand, system integrators, modules, chips/devices, equipment, materials, infrastructure.
- Scarce-layer evidence: supplier count, qualification time, capacity expansion difficulty, purity/yield/reliability constraints, customer certification, lead times.
- Candidate universe: at least a small cross-layer list for quick scans; larger lists for deep scans.
- Source quality for finalists: filings, exchange docs, company IR, transcripts, patents, standards, official orders, regulatory/project docs, credible trade sources.

## Evidence Grading

- **Strong**: Primary source or directly verifiable company/regulatory/filing evidence.
- **Medium**: Reputable financial media, trade publication, analyst transcript, or cross-checked secondary source.
- **Weak**: Social media, unsourced commentary, one-off rumor, or promotional material.
- **Missing**: Important fact not found or not provided.

## Confidence Rules

- Cap confidence at 40 when current price/valuation is missing for a valuation-sensitive conclusion.
- Cap confidence at 50 when the latest financials are older than one full reporting cycle.
- Cap confidence at 60 when only secondary sources support a key customer, contract, or supply-chain claim.
- Cap confidence at 70 when valuation is approximate but business evidence is strong.
- Allow confidence above 80 only when primary evidence supports the key facts and the panel's disagreement is narrow.

## Missing Evidence Language

Use direct language:

- "This is a provisional view because current valuation is missing."
- "The thesis depends on customer concentration, but the evidence packet does not include customer disclosure."
- "I would not raise confidence until the latest filing, transcript, and debt schedule are checked."
- "The supply-chain claim is a lead, not proof, until a primary source confirms the company's role."
