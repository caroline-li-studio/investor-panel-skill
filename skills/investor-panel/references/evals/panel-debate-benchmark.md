# Panel Debate Benchmark

Use this benchmark to test whether Investor Panel produces useful disagreement and research priorities.

## Goal

The panel should not merely average opinions. It should expose conflicts among quality, price, growth, macro regime, statistical evidence, supply-chain proof, and tail risk.

## Debate Quality Rubric

| Dimension | Strong Output |
|---|---|
| Consensus | Identifies 2-4 evidence-backed points most lenses accept |
| Disagreement | Names where high-quality lenses truly conflict |
| Veto power | Lets one lens block or downgrade the thesis when its risk is decisive |
| Evidence gaps | Separates missing facts from assumptions |
| Falsifiability | Gives 3-5 assumptions that can be checked later |
| Next checks | Points to filings, metrics, sources, or events that would resolve disagreement |

## Required Test Set

Run at least 10 company or synthetic cases across these categories:

1. High-quality but high-valuation compounder.
2. Cheap company with possible permanent decline.
3. Cyclical company near peak margins.
4. Loss-making high-growth technology company.
5. Financial or insurance company with opaque balance-sheet risk.
6. Special situation or activist catalyst.
7. Distressed company with capital-structure complexity.
8. Emerging-market compounder with governance risk.
9. Supply-chain bottleneck candidate in a hot theme.
10. Macro-sensitive winner with crowded positioning.

## Expected Disagreements

- Buffett/Munger/Terry Smith may like business quality while Damodaran rejects valuation.
- Graham/Klarman may reject a great compounder that lacks a margin of safety.
- Cathie Wood may accept innovation optionality that Graham rejects and Taleb stress-tests.
- Druckenmiller may like a setup while Buffett says it is outside long-term ownership criteria.
- Serenity may rank a theme company low if it is adjacent to, not owner of, the bottleneck.
- Simons may return insufficient data when narrative lenses are confident.
- Marks may shift from offense to defense when cycle compensation is poor.

## Pass Criteria

- At least 30% of cases produce substantive disagreement.
- Every deep panel lists loaded deep packs and modules.
- Every deep panel includes at least 3 falsifiable assumptions.
- No investor lens impersonates the real person.
- No current factual claim is invented without evidence.
- At least one case correctly returns `too-hard` or `insufficient evidence`.

## Review Checklist

Before accepting an output:

1. Does each lens have a different core reason?
2. Are confidence scores capped when evidence is missing?
3. Does the strongest bearish case survive even when the final verdict is positive?
4. Does the strongest bullish case survive even when the final verdict is cautious?
5. Are next checks specific enough for a researcher to execute?
6. Would the output still be useful if no trade were placed?
