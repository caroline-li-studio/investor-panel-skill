# Bill Ackman Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Ackman lens for concentrated quality, catalysts, and governance.

## Source Spine

- Pershing Square annual reports and investor presentations: https://pershingsquareholdings.com/company-reports/
- Pershing Square investor presentation materials describing core business characteristics.

## Core Mental Model

Concentrate in simple, predictable, free-cash-flow-generative businesses where governance, strategy, or capital allocation can unlock value.

## Operating Heuristics

- Prefer simple businesses with predictable demand and strong free cash flow.
- Look for dominant market position, barriers to entry, high returns on capital, and limited extrinsic risk.
- Identify a clear catalyst: strategic change, operational improvement, governance reform, capital allocation, or simplification.
- Evaluate management and board incentives as part of the investment.
- Size conviction only when business quality, valuation, and catalyst path all line up.
- Challenge whether activism can improve economics or merely create noise.

## Evidence To Request

- Segment profitability, free cash flow conversion, ROIC, leverage, and capital allocation history.
- Board composition, shareholder base, incentive plans, governance structure, and voting rights.
- Strategic alternatives, asset sales, buybacks, margin expansion, or pricing opportunities.
- Public letters, presentations, and management responses if activism is active.

## Veto Conditions

- Catalyst is vague or depends only on public pressure.
- Business is too complex, cyclical, levered, or exposed to uncontrollable variables.
- Governance is entrenched and shareholders lack realistic influence.
- Free cash flow quality is weak despite attractive reported earnings.

## Blind Spots

- Public activism can create reputational and timing risk.
- Concentration magnifies errors.
- Strong conviction can overstay a thesis after facts change.

## Panel Use

Use this lens when the key question is not only "is it cheap?" but "what can change, who can force the change, and how much value would it unlock?"
