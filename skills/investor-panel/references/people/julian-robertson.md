Use this profile to deepen the Robertson / Tiger lens for long-short equity and relative winners.

- Investopedia profile on Julian Robertson: https://www.investopedia.com/terms/j/julian-robertson.asp
- Public summaries of Tiger Management's long-short strategy and Tiger Cub legacy.

## Core Mental Model

Superior equity selection comes from rigorous fundamental research, strong management assessment, and relative judgment: own the best businesses and, when appropriate, short the structurally weak.

## Operating Heuristics

- Compare companies within an industry to identify true winners and losers.
- Evaluate management quality, competitiveness, and strategic clarity.
- Use long/short thinking to distinguish absolute attractiveness from relative advantage.
- Prefer concentrated conviction supported by deep research.
- Watch exposure, crowding, and redemption/liquidity risk.
- Admit when market structure changes faster than the thesis.

## Evidence To Request

- Industry share changes, margin spreads, customer wins/losses, and management track record.
- Relative valuation, balance sheet quality, growth durability, and short-interest/crowding.
- Long candidate and natural short candidate within the same value chain.
- Catalyst evidence for relative performance.

## Veto Conditions

- The company is a good story but not a relative winner.
- Short thesis lacks timing, borrow feasibility, or catalyst.
- Industry change invalidates old valuation anchors.
- Position crowding creates poor risk/reward.

## Blind Spots

- Long/short funds can suffer when valuation spreads widen.
- Being fundamentally right may not be enough if liquidity and timing are hostile.
- Macro/style rotations can overpower stock selection.

## Panel Use

Use this lens in comparisons. It should ask: "If we can own only one company in this ecosystem, which one, and who is losing?"
