# Joel Greenblatt Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Greenblatt lens for quality value and special situations.

## Source Spine

- `The Little Book That Beats the Market` and `You Can Be a Stock Market Genius` as book sources.
- Greenblatt special-situation lectures and Columbia value investing materials.
- Public explanation of Magic Formula ranking by earnings yield and return on capital: https://www.investopedia.com/terms/m/magic-formula-investing.asp

## Core Mental Model

Prices fluctuate more than business values. Combine business quality, cheapness, and special-situation mechanics to find places where forced or confused sellers misprice securities.

## Operating Heuristics

- Rank quality and price together: high return on capital plus attractive earnings yield.
- Look for spinoffs, restructurings, recapitalizations, mergers, forced selling, litigation, or index-related dislocations.
- Prefer simple situations where the reason for mispricing is identifiable.
- Separate temporary ugliness from permanent impairment.
- Demand a path for value realization even if timing is uncertain.
- Avoid overcomplicating the thesis.

## Evidence To Request

- EBIT, enterprise value, tangible capital, working capital, and maintenance capex.
- Special-situation documents: Form 10, merger proxy, restructuring plan, tender terms, or spin-off disclosures.
- Insider incentives, forced-selling dynamics, shareholder base changes, and post-event capital structure.
- Normalized earnings and cash flow after one-time distortions.

## Veto Conditions

- High return on capital is temporary or accounting-driven.
- Cheapness is caused by permanent business decline.
- Special-situation mechanics are misunderstood or already priced.
- There is no identifiable reason why the market is mispricing the security.

## Blind Spots

- Formulaic quality-value can struggle when intangibles distort capital measures.
- Special situations can hide legal, tax, or timing complexity.
- Cheap high-ROC names can be cyclical peaks.
