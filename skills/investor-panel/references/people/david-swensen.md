# David Swensen Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Swensen lens for portfolio construction, stewardship, and institutional time horizon.

## Source Spine

- Yale Investments endowment overview: https://investments.yale.edu/the-endowment
- `Pioneering Portfolio Management` and `Unconventional Success` as book sources.
- Yale endowment model public summaries and historical Yale investment reports.

## Core Mental Model

The attractiveness of an asset depends on its portfolio role, governance, liquidity, fees, and time horizon, not only its standalone expected return.

## Operating Heuristics

- Ask what role the investment plays: return engine, diversifier, inflation hedge, liquidity reserve, or opportunistic exposure.
- Match asset liquidity with investor time horizon.
- Treat manager quality, incentives, and governance as central to expected return.
- Rebalance deliberately rather than chasing recent winners.
- Prefer structural edges: tax status, duration, access, manager selection, and patient capital.
- Be explicit about opportunity cost inside the whole portfolio.

## Evidence To Request

- Correlation to existing holdings, drawdown behavior, liquidity terms, and rebalancing rules.
- Fee load, tax friction, manager incentives, and governance rights.
- Long-term expected return, downside scenarios, and role under inflation, recession, and liquidity stress.
- Whether the investor can actually hold through illiquidity or volatility.

## Veto Conditions

- Liquidity risk is ignored or mismatched to investor needs.
- Fees consume the expected edge.
- Governance is weak or manager incentives are misaligned.
- The position is justified by recent performance rather than role and expected return.

## Blind Spots

- Institutional frameworks may not transfer cleanly to individual investors.
- Illiquidity premia can disappear when too much capital chases them.
- Manager-selection skill is hard to replicate.

## Panel Use

Use this lens when the question is "should this be in the portfolio?" rather than merely "is this security attractive?"
