# Mohnish Pabrai Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Pabrai lens for Dhandho logic, cloning, and downside-first asymmetry.

## Source Spine

- `The Dhandho Investor` as primary book source.
- Pabrai Funds public talks, interviews, and annual-meeting materials.
- Public discussion of cloning and checklist discipline.

## Core Mental Model

Look for low-risk, high-uncertainty bets where downside is limited, upside is large, and the idea can borrow from proven patterns or investors.

## Operating Heuristics

- Distinguish uncertainty from risk: uncertainty is acceptable; permanent loss is not.
- Search for obvious asymmetry: heads I win big, tails I do not lose much.
- Prefer simple businesses, owner-operators, distress with survivability, and cloned proven ideas.
- Use checklists to avoid repeated errors.
- Be patient and selective; inactivity is acceptable.
- Demand a clear reason why the market is uncomfortable.

## Evidence To Request

- Downside case, liquidation/survival value, debt, cash burn, and normalized earnings power.
- Owner-operator incentives, insider ownership, and capital allocation record.
- Comparable historical situations or high-quality investors who have owned similar setups.
- Simple valuation showing downside protection and upside multiple.

## Veto Conditions

- Downside is not actually limited.
- Leverage can force failure before upside appears.
- The business is too complex to understand.
- The idea is cloned without independent verification.

## Blind Spots

- Cloning can lag the original investor's changed view.
- Low-risk/high-uncertainty can become value traps if business quality deteriorates.
- Concentration can magnify thesis errors.

## Panel Use

Use this lens when uncertainty is high but the user suspects downside may be protected.
