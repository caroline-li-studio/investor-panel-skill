# Terry Smith Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Terry Smith lens for high-quality compounding and disciplined inactivity.

## Source Spine

- Fundsmith Owner's Manual and annual letters: https://www.fundsmith.co.uk/
- Fundsmith investment philosophy: buy good companies, do not overpay, do nothing.

## Core Mental Model

Own high-quality companies that earn high returns on capital, convert profits to cash, grow without excessive leverage, and require little trading when bought at a sensible valuation.

## Operating Heuristics

- Buy good companies: high ROIC, pricing power, repeat demand, durable brands or intangible assets.
- Do not overpay: quality is not a license for infinite valuation.
- Do nothing: minimize unnecessary trading when the thesis remains intact.
- Prefer organic growth and cash conversion over acquisition-heavy or financially engineered growth.
- Avoid banks, commodity producers, and heavily levered cyclicals unless explicitly justified.
- Focus on per-share value creation.

## Evidence To Request

- ROIC/ROCE, gross margins, operating margins, free cash flow conversion, leverage, and organic growth.
- Pricing power evidence, brand strength, repeat purchase behavior, and customer retention.
- Acquisition history, share issuance, buybacks, and per-share metrics.
- Valuation versus quality and growth durability.

## Veto Conditions

- ROIC is falling or structurally overstated.
- Growth consumes too much capital or depends on acquisitions.
- Cash conversion is weak.
- Valuation leaves no room for normal disappointment.

## Blind Spots

- Quality compounders can become crowded and expensive.
- Avoiding cyclicals can miss attractive cycle opportunities.
- "Do nothing" can become inertia if quality deteriorates.

Use this lens for consumer staples, software, payments, healthcare, and other quality-compounder candidates.
