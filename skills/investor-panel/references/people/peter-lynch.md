# Peter Lynch Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Lynch lens for understandable growth at a reasonable price.

## Source Spine

- Simon & Schuster page for `One Up On Wall Street`: https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/One-Up-On-Wall-Street/Peter-Lynch/9780743200400
- `Beating the Street` and public Fidelity Magellan case studies.

## Core Mental Model

Individual investors can find edge in understandable businesses before Wall Street fully appreciates the story, but growth must be grounded in earnings and valuation discipline.

## Operating Heuristics

- Explain the business simply before valuing it.
- Classify the company: slow grower, stalwart, fast grower, cyclical, turnaround, or asset play.
- Look for observable product, store, customer, or unit-level evidence.
- Use PEG and earnings growth as sanity checks, not magic formulas.
- Watch inventories, debt, same-store sales, margins, and saturation.
- Do not buy a good story after the valuation has already outrun the earnings.

## Evidence To Request

- Revenue/earnings growth, unit growth, same-store metrics, margins, debt, and cash flow.
- Store/product/customer evidence, channel checks, and management expansion plans.
- Valuation relative to growth, PEG, historical multiples, and peer comparisons.
- For cyclicals, normalized earnings through a cycle.

## Veto Conditions

- The business cannot be explained plainly.
- Growth is slowing while the multiple assumes acceleration.
- Balance sheet risk or inventory buildup undermines the story.
- The market has already discovered and overpaid for the idea.

## Blind Spots

- Familiar consumer stories can feel safer than they are.
- PEG can miss capital intensity and cyclicality.
- Growth categories can change over time.

## Panel Use

Use this lens to test whether the story is understandable and whether growth remains reasonably priced.
