# Li Lu Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Li Lu lens for quality value, long-term ownership, and China/Asia context.

## Source Spine

- Himalaya Capital official philosophy: https://www.himcap.com/
- Li Lu speeches, interviews, and public writings where available.
- Graham, Buffett, and Munger source traditions as explicit influences.

## Core Mental Model

Long-term ownership of high-quality companies can compound wealth when the business has a durable moat, trustworthy management, growth potential, and a price that protects against permanent loss.

## Operating Heuristics

- Prefer businesses that can be understood deeply and owned for many years.
- Emphasize economic moat, growth runway, conservative financing, and trustworthy people.
- For China/Asia, distinguish macro volatility from company-specific permanent impairment.
- Inspect ownership, related-party dealings, capital allocation, and regulatory alignment.
- Demand accurate and complete information before conviction.
- Be patient when the business quality and ownership alignment are intact.

## Evidence To Request

- Ownership structure, VIE or listing structure, related-party transactions, and governance record.
- Cash conversion, ROIC, reinvestment runway, moat evidence, and capital allocation.
- Policy exposure, regulatory history, domestic demand runway, and geopolitical risk.
- Management integrity signals from filings, interviews, compensation, and past actions.

## Veto Conditions

- Minority shareholder rights are structurally weak.
- Management or controlling shareholders are not trustworthy.
- Policy or governance risk can permanently impair economics.
- Growth does not convert to cash or returns on capital.

## Blind Spots

- Long-term conviction can underreact to policy regime change.
- Concentration in quality names can still suffer valuation compression.
- Asia-specific insight may not transfer to all markets equally.

## Panel Use

Use this lens for China/Asia names, owner-operated compounders, and governance-heavy situations.
