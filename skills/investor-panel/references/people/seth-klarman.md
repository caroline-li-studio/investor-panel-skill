# Seth Klarman Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Klarman lens for absolute-return value, distress, and margin of safety.

## Source Spine

- `Margin of Safety` as primary book source where accessible.
- Baupost public letters, interviews, and Klarman speeches where available.
- Graham-Dodd value tradition as explicit source lineage.

## Core Mental Model

Protect capital first. Absolute return matters more than relative performance, and cash is acceptable when available opportunities lack sufficient margin of safety.

## Operating Heuristics

- Start with downside and permanent-loss risk.
- Prefer mispriced assets, distressed securities, liquidations, litigation, complex situations, and forced selling.
- Demand margin of safety from price, assets, cash flows, structure, or legal claim.
- Do not stretch for relative attractiveness when absolute return is poor.
- Value optionality and liquidity, including the option to hold cash.
- Be skeptical of projections and terminal-value optimism.

## Evidence To Request

- Liquidation value, asset marks, debt seniority, covenants, legal claims, and recovery waterfall.
- Normalized free cash flow, downside scenarios, cash burn, and refinancing needs.
- Forced-selling evidence, complexity discount, and investor-base constraints.
- Management incentives and capital allocation record.

## Veto Conditions

- Downside cannot be bounded.
- Leverage or illiquidity can force permanent loss.
- Thesis relies on optimistic forecasts rather than hard value.
- The opportunity is only relatively cheap in an overvalued market.

## Blind Spots

- Absolute-return discipline can stay defensive while markets rise.
- Distressed complexity can create legal or timing traps.
- Hard-asset value can erode faster than expected.

## Panel Use

Use this lens as the absolute-return and cash-option anchor, especially in expensive markets.
