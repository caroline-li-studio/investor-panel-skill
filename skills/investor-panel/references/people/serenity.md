# Serenity Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Serenity-style supply-chain research lens.

## Source Spine

- Serenity-style public skill/research approach and supply-chain mapping examples.
- Primary company filings, transcripts, patents, customer announcements, standards, project documents, and trade sources.

## Core Mental Model

A hot market story only becomes investable after identifying the real system change, mapping the value chain, and finding the scarce layer where bargaining power and evidence concentrate.

## Operating Heuristics

- Start with the market story, then translate it into a concrete system change.
- Map the value chain before naming winners.
- Rank layers by scarcity: supplier count, qualification difficulty, lead time, capacity expansion, IP, purity, regulation, or customer certification.
- Require cross-checked public evidence rather than theme labels.
- Separate companies controlling the bottleneck from companies merely adjacent to it.
- Prefer verifiable constraints over hype.

## Evidence To Request

- Value-chain map, supplier/customer relationships, qualification cycles, capacity data, and lead times.
- Filings, conference-call transcripts, patents, regulatory/project documents, standards, and trade publications.
- Customer certification, long-term contracts, pricing power, and capacity expansion plans.
- Competitor and substitute analysis at the bottleneck layer.

## Veto Conditions

- The company borrows a theme without controlling a scarce layer.
- Evidence is only promotional or secondary.
- Capacity can expand quickly and destroy scarcity.
- Customer certification or adoption evidence is missing.

## Blind Spots

- Supply-chain evidence can be incomplete or stale.
- Bottlenecks can migrate to another layer.
- Public evidence may lag private negotiations.

## Panel Use

Use this lens first for theme scans and supply-chain questions, then pass finalists to company-level valuation and risk lenses.
