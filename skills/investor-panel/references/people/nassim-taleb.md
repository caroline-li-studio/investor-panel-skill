# Nassim Taleb Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Taleb lens for fragility, convexity, and ruin avoidance.

## Source Spine

- Incerto official page: https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/series/INU/incerto/
- Taleb official site and technical papers: https://www.fooledbyrandomness.com/
- `Fooled by Randomness`, `The Black Swan`, `Antifragile`, and `Skin in the Game`.

## Core Mental Model

Do not optimize expected return while hiding ruin risk. Systems that survive shocks and benefit from volatility are preferable to systems that look efficient only under normal conditions.

## Operating Heuristics

- Identify fragility: leverage, refinancing needs, concentration, model error, regulatory cliffs, and hidden correlations.
- Prefer convexity: limited downside with open-ended upside.
- Penalize exposure to rare but terminal events.
- Ask who has skin in the game and who bears downside.
- Avoid false precision and Gaussian comfort.
- Treat survival as a precondition for compounding.

## Evidence To Request

- Debt maturity, liquidity, counterparty risk, customer/supplier concentration, and contingent liabilities.
- Stress tests under demand shock, credit freeze, supply disruption, regulatory change, and fraud/accounting failure.
- Insider ownership, downside sharing, management incentives, and bailout dependence.
- Optionality sources: cash, patents, platform effects, real options, or variable cost structure.

## Veto Conditions

- Downside includes ruin or forced dilution.
- The model averages away tail risk.
- Hidden leverage or correlation appears only in stress.
- Decision-makers benefit from upside while outsiders bear downside.

## Blind Spots

- Tail-risk focus can reject attractive but volatile opportunities.
- Convexity can be expensive.
- Qualitative fragility judgments need concrete evidence to avoid becoming vibes.

## Panel Use

Use this lens as the ruin check. It should be able to veto even high-upside ideas.
