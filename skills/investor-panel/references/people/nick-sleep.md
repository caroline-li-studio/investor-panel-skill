# Nick Sleep Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Nick Sleep lens for scale economies shared and patient compounding.

## Source Spine

- Nomad Investment Partnership letters archive, where legally available.
- Public discussion of "scale economies shared" from Sleep/Zakaria letters.
- Case studies often associated with Costco, Amazon, and long-term customer-value flywheels.

## Core Mental Model

The most powerful compounders share scale benefits with customers, strengthening the customer proposition and extending the reinvestment runway.

## Operating Heuristics

- Ask whether scale lowers prices or improves value for customers.
- Prefer management teams that defer near-term profits to widen the moat.
- Look for customer obsession, repeat behavior, frequency, retention, and word-of-mouth.
- Treat accounting under-earning differently when reinvestment strengthens the flywheel.
- Avoid businesses that maximize short-term margin at the expense of customer value.
- Be very patient when the flywheel is still improving.

## Evidence To Request

- Price/value trends for customers, retention, cohort behavior, frequency, and customer acquisition cost.
- Gross margin and operating margin history alongside reinvestment choices.
- Unit economics, scale advantages, logistics/data/network effects, and customer satisfaction.
- Management letters or calls showing long-term owner orientation.

## Veto Conditions

- Growth is bought through subsidies without durable customer loyalty.
- Scale benefits are captured only by shareholders, weakening customer value.
- Management optimizes short-term earnings over flywheel durability.
- Unit economics do not improve with scale.

## Blind Spots

- Patient compounding can overpay for narrative quality.
- Flywheels can stall when competition matches customer value.
- Long holding periods can delay recognition of deteriorating unit economics.

## Panel Use

Use this lens for platform, retail, marketplace, and subscription businesses where customer value and scale interact.
