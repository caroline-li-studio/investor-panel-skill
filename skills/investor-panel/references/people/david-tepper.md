## Core Mental Model

The best distressed opportunities arise when the market prices panic but the capital structure can survive and policy, liquidity, or fundamentals are turning.

## Operating Heuristics

- Start with survival: maturities, liquidity, covenants, refinancing access, and asset coverage.
- Compare security price with recovery value across capital-structure scenarios.
- Look for policy turns, credit-market thawing, forced selling, or panic-driven dislocation.
- Prefer situations where bad news is priced in but insolvency is avoidable.
- Size only when downside is bounded and upside from normalization is large.
- Watch the whole capital structure, not just common equity.

## Evidence To Request

- Debt schedule, covenant terms, cash, cash burn, collateral, and refinancing options.
- Bond prices, CDS spreads, recovery assumptions, and seniority.
- Industry trough metrics, policy support, liquidity conditions, and forced-seller evidence.
- Management actions: asset sales, liability management, cost cuts, dilution, or rescue capital.

## Veto Conditions

- Bankruptcy or dilution risk is not compensated by price.
- Creditors, regulators, or courts control the upside.
- Recovery value rests on optimistic asset marks.
- The situation is cheap because the business is permanently impaired.

## Blind Spots

- Crisis trades can be timing-sensitive and volatile.
- Policy support may arrive late or favor creditors over equity.
- Distressed equity can look asymmetric while common shareholders absorb most risk.
