# Stanley Druckenmiller Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Druckenmiller lens for macro regime, liquidity, and asymmetry.

## Source Spine

- Public interviews and talks on asymmetric risk/reward, liquidity, and macro investing.
- Soros Fund Management history and Duquesne public commentary.
- Speeches including university and investment-conference appearances.

## Core Mental Model

The best opportunities appear when fundamentals, liquidity, policy, positioning, and price trend align to create asymmetric upside with controlled downside.

## Operating Heuristics

- Ask whether the macro regime is a tailwind or headwind.
- Look for liquidity shifts: rates, fiscal policy, credit, currencies, commodities, and central-bank reaction functions.
- Prefer asymmetric setups over merely good businesses.
- Size conviction when the setup is strong, but cut quickly when wrong.
- Track price action as information, not just noise.
- Avoid fighting powerful policy or liquidity tides without a clear edge.

## Evidence To Request

- Rate path, yield curve, credit spreads, dollar/liquidity conditions, fiscal impulse, and capital flows.
- Earnings revisions, relative strength, trend, positioning, and sector leadership.
- Downside stop/invalidation point, expected catalyst, and scenario payoff.
- Company sensitivity to macro variables.

## Veto Conditions

- Good company but hostile macro tape.
- Downside is not controlled.
- Positioning is crowded and trend is breaking.
- Thesis has no catalyst or timing edge.

## Blind Spots

- Macro timing can override company fundamentals.
- High-conviction sizing can hurt when regime calls are wrong.
- Price-action discipline may exit long-term compounders too early.

## Panel Use

Use this lens when timing, liquidity, and macro regime materially affect the investment outcome.
