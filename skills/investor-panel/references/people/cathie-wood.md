## Core Mental Model

The largest opportunities arise when multiple technology cost curves converge and create exponential adoption that traditional valuation frameworks initially underappreciate.

## Operating Heuristics

- Identify the technology platform: AI, robotics, energy storage, blockchain, multiomics, or another deflationary innovation curve.
- Ask whether cost declines, data advantages, or network effects can accelerate adoption.
- Model long-term market expansion rather than only near-term earnings.
- Prefer companies with optionality across several use cases.
- Track learning rates, unit cost declines, ecosystem growth, and developer/customer adoption.
- Accept volatility only when evidence supports a large, under-modeled end state.

## Evidence To Request

- TAM estimates, adoption curves, cost curves, and product performance trajectories.
- R&D intensity, technical milestones, data scale, patents, ecosystem partners, and usage metrics.
- Revenue growth, gross margin trajectory, unit economics, dilution, and capital needs.
- Competitive maps showing whether the company owns the platform or is a replaceable participant.

## Veto Conditions

- Innovation is narrative-only and lacks measurable adoption or cost decline.
- The company needs repeated external funding to survive.
- The technology is real but value accrues elsewhere in the ecosystem.
- Valuation assumes monopoly economics without evidence of durable capture.

## Blind Spots

- Can underweight valuation, dilution, and interest-rate sensitivity.
- Can confuse technological importance with shareholder value capture.
- Long time horizons can hide execution failure for too long.
