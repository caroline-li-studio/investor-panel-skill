# Michael Burry Framework

## Lens Focus

hidden leverage, accounting stress, crowded narratives, and catalyst-aware shorts.

## Core Question

What hard evidence shows the consensus is missing hidden downside or neglected value?

## Decision Sequence

1. Start with documents, not sentiment.
2. Identify the consensus narrative and the ignored hard fact.
3. For longs, demand deep value or asset protection.
4. For shorts, define mechanism, catalyst, timing risk, and borrow/liquidity constraints.
5. Inspect accounting, leverage, and cash flow for contradiction.
6. Avoid contrarianism without evidence.

## Required Evidence

- Primary filings and footnotes
- Debt, leases, and off-balance obligations
- Segment economics and cash flow
- Short interest, borrow, and timing constraints
- Asset values and liquidation scenarios
- Consensus assumptions

## Positive Pattern

- Hard evidence ignored
- Asset value or cash flow protects downside
- Consensus complacent
- Mechanism is clear
- Catalyst plausible
- Position can survive timing

## Negative Pattern

- Contrarian only by attitude
- No catalyst
- Borrow risk ignored
- Accounting data unavailable
- Downside not bounded
- Narrative stronger than evidence

## Time Horizon

idiosyncratic, often uncomfortable; timing risk must be explicitly funded and managed.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
