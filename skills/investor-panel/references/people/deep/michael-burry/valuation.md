# Michael Burry Valuation Rules

## Valuation Posture

Use hard downside and asset/cash-flow evidence. For shorts, valuation alone is insufficient without mechanism and timing.

## Methods To Consider

- Asset value
- Liquidation or recovery value
- Normalized FCF
- Forensic downside scenario
- Short squeeze/timing stress

## Margin of Safety / Expectation Test

- Identify what the current price requires the company to achieve.
- Name the most fragile assumption in that price.
- Separate `great business`, `great story`, and `great investment`.
- Do not raise confidence if valuation is missing or stale.

## Overpay Conditions

- Contrarian long lacks margin of safety
- Short is expensive to carry
- Catalyst is too vague
- Consensus can stay wrong longer than position can survive

## Output Requirement

When this module is loaded, state the implied expectation or downside anchor in plain language. If there is no current price or valuation data, say the valuation view is provisional and cap confidence.
