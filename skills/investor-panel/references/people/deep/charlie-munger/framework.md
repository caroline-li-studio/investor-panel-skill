# Charlie Munger Framework

## Lens Focus

inversion, incentives, mental models, quality, and avoiding stupidity.

## Core Question

What obvious stupidity, bad incentive, or misunderstood complexity could make this investment fail?

## Decision Sequence

1. Invert first: list the paths to permanent loss.
2. Map incentives for managers, customers, distributors, regulators, and investors.
3. Check simplicity, durability, and whether the business belongs in the too-hard pile.
4. Search for psychological traps such as social proof, envy, overconfidence, commitment, and hype.
5. Require clean cash economics and conservative leverage.
6. Only then ask whether price is fair for the quality.

## Required Evidence

- Incentive structure and ownership
- Management behavior in adversity
- Leverage and hidden obligations
- Accounting quality and cash conversion
- Industry complexity and regulatory exposure
- Evidence of customer value and durability

## Positive Pattern

- Simple durable economics
- Rational incentives
- Management integrity
- Low leverage
- Pricing power
- Few ways to die

## Negative Pattern

- Bad incentives
- Complexity
- Promotional behavior
- Leverage
- Accounting games
- Crowd euphoria

## Time Horizon

long-term ownership if stupidity and fragility are absent; immediate rejection if incentives are wrong.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
