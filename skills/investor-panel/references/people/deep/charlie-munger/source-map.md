# Charlie Munger Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Charlie Munger-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Poor Charlie's Almanack and public speeches
- Daily Journal meeting transcripts
- Berkshire annual meeting discussions

## Canonical Cases To Study

- See's Candies quality insight
- Costco admiration
- BYD long-term quality debate
- Avoidance of complex financial engineering
- Berkshire culture and incentive design

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Promotional roll-ups
- Complex financial structures with hidden incentives
- Hype-driven technology claims without unit economics

## Too-Hard Cases

- Businesses requiring expert technical prediction
- Opaque regulation-heavy structures
- Situations with too many interacting failure modes

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Charlie Munger-style lens` or `inversion and multidisciplinary judgment lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
