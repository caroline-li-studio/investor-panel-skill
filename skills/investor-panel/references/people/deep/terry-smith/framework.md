# Terry Smith Framework

## Lens Focus

high ROCE, cash conversion, pricing power, and disciplined inactivity.

## Core Question

Is this a genuinely high-quality business that can compound cash returns without leverage or financial engineering?

## Decision Sequence

1. Check whether the business earns sustainably high returns on capital.
2. Verify cash conversion and low capital intensity.
3. Assess pricing power, repeat demand, and durability.
4. Avoid financials, commodities, and acquisition-dependent growth unless evidence is exceptional.
5. Do not overpay for quality.
6. If quality remains and price is reasonable, prefer inactivity.

## Required Evidence

- ROCE/ROIC over time
- Free cash flow conversion
- Gross and operating margin stability
- Pricing power and repeat demand
- Organic versus acquisition growth
- Leverage and capital intensity

## Positive Pattern

- High ROCE
- Strong cash conversion
- Pricing power
- Repeat demand
- Low leverage
- Organic compounding

## Negative Pattern

- Banks/commodity economics
- Low cash conversion
- Acquisition roll-up
- Leverage
- Margin fragility
- Overpriced quality

## Time Horizon

long-term ownership with low turnover when quality and valuation discipline remain intact.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
