# Terry Smith Business Quality Rules

## Definition of Quality

Quality is consistently high returns on capital, strong cash conversion, durable demand, and minimal need for leverage or heroic reinvestment.

## What To Inspect

- Is ROCE persistently high?
- Does profit become free cash flow?
- Can the company raise prices without losing customers?
- Is growth organic or bought?

## Industry Fit

Best for consumer, healthcare, software-like repeat demand, and branded/network businesses; cautious on banks, insurers, commodity producers, and cyclicals.

## Moat and Customer Economics

- Identify the specific source of customer value or bargaining power.
- Distinguish durable advantage from temporary cycle benefit, subsidy, or popularity.
- Ask whether the advantage can survive competition, regulation, technology change, and capital inflows.
- Lower confidence when the business quality claim cannot be verified outside management narrative.

## Distinctive Questions

- What fact would make this particular lens lose interest first?
- What evidence would this lens demand before raising confidence above 70?
- Where would this lens disagree with a generic quality investor?
