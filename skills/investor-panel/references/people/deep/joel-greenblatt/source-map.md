# Joel Greenblatt Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Joel Greenblatt-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- You Can Be a Stock Market Genius
- The Little Book That Beats the Market
- Public lectures and special-situation case studies

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Spinoffs
- Restructurings
- Recapitalizations
- Magic Formula style baskets
- Hidden asset situations

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Low ROIC cheap stocks
- Accounting earnings without cash
- Special situations with no value-unlocking mechanics

## Too-Hard Cases

- Financial statements too adjusted
- Regulatory special situations without documents
- Situation mechanics unclear

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Joel Greenblatt-style lens` or `quality value and special situations lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
