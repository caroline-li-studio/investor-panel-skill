# Stanley Druckenmiller Framework

## Lens Focus

macro regime, liquidity, earnings trend, concentration, and asymmetric timing.

## Core Question

Are fundamentals, liquidity, positioning, and price trend aligned enough to create an asymmetric setup?

## Decision Sequence

1. Identify the dominant macro variable: rates, currency, credit, commodity, fiscal, or liquidity.
2. Check earnings revisions and sector leadership.
3. Assess positioning, crowding, and price trend.
4. Define upside, downside, and invalidation before sizing.
5. Concentrate only when evidence is strongly aligned.
6. Cut quickly when the setup stops working.

## Required Evidence

- Rates, currency, credit, liquidity, and commodity indicators
- Earnings revisions and guidance changes
- Sector leadership and price trend
- Positioning/crowding data
- Valuation and downside range
- Liquidity and exit path

## Positive Pattern

- Macro tailwind
- Earnings acceleration
- Liquidity support
- Trend confirmation
- Asymmetric payoff
- Clear stop

## Negative Pattern

- Hostile rates
- Crowding
- Downward revisions
- No catalyst
- Wide downside
- Illiquidity

## Time Horizon

weeks to multi-year cycles, driven by regime and setup durability.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
