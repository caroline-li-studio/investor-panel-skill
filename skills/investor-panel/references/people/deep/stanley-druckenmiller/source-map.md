# Stanley Druckenmiller Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Stanley Druckenmiller-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Public interviews and conference talks
- Duquesne history and macro case discussions
- Market commentary on liquidity, rates, and trend following

## Canonical Cases To Study

- British pound short with Soros
- Technology and liquidity cycles
- Concentrated macro equity winners
- Rates-driven regime shifts
- Risk reduction when wrong

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Good company in hostile liquidity regime
- Crowded trade with no asymmetry
- Thesis with unclear stop or invalidation

## Too-Hard Cases

- No identifiable macro driver
- Illiquid position without exit path
- Company fundamentals detached from trade horizon

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Stanley Druckenmiller-style lens` or `macro asymmetry lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
