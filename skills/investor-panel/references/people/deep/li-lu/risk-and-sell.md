# Li Lu Risk and Sell Rules

## Primary Risks

- Governance impairment
- Policy risk
- Related-party extraction
- Cash trapped away from shareholders
- Moat overstated

## Kill Criteria

- Minority treatment worsens
- Policy permanently impairs economics
- Cash conversion fails
- Owner alignment breaks
- Valuation no longer protects capital

## Thesis-Breaking Facts

- The core evidence named by this lens reverses or fails to appear.
- The investment becomes dependent on assumptions outside this lens's competence.
- Downside expands while upside becomes more dependent on hope or multiple expansion.
- New evidence shows management, balance sheet, or industry structure is worse than assumed.

## Hold / Sell Review

Ask these questions:

1. Is the original reason for owning still true?
2. Has valuation consumed the expected return?
3. Has risk increased faster than evidence improved?
4. Is there a simpler or better-understood opportunity with superior risk-adjusted return?
5. Would this lens initiate the position today with the same evidence?

## Confidence Caps

- Cap confidence at 50 if the risk depends on missing primary filings or debt details.
- Cap confidence at 60 if the sell case depends on a catalyst with uncertain timing.
- Cap confidence at 70 if the business evidence is strong but current valuation is stale.
