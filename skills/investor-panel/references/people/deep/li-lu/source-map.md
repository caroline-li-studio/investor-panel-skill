# Li Lu Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Li Lu-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Li Lu public lectures and talks
- Himalaya public materials where available
- Long-term quality value case studies with China/Asia context

## Canonical Cases To Study

- BYD-style long-run quality debate
- Asian consumer compounders
- Owner-led businesses
- Modernization infrastructure winners
- Governance-improving companies

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Opaque related-party structures
- Policy risk that can permanently impair economics
- Headline growth without cash conversion

## Too-Hard Cases

- Ownership structure unverifiable
- Minority shareholder treatment unclear
- Policy boundary unknowable

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Li Lu-style lens` or `quality value and Asia governance lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
