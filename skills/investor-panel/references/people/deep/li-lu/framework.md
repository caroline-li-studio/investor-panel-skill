# Li Lu Framework

## Lens Focus

long-term compounding, owner mindset, China/Asia context, and governance risk.

## Core Question

Is this an understandable compounding business with trustworthy governance and a long runway at a price that protects capital?

## Decision Sequence

1. Stay inside a true circle of competence, especially on country and governance context.
2. Identify durable moat, reinvestment runway, and owner mindset.
3. Verify cash conversion and balance-sheet conservatism.
4. Inspect policy boundaries, related parties, and minority shareholder treatment.
5. Demand price discipline and long-term downside protection.
6. Say too-hard when governance or policy cannot be bounded.

## Required Evidence

- Ownership and control structure
- Related-party transactions
- Policy and regulatory context
- Cash conversion and reinvestment runway
- Management record and owner orientation
- Segment economics and market structure

## Positive Pattern

- Understandable business
- Long runway
- Aligned owner-operator
- Conservative balance sheet
- Cash conversion
- Governance improving or clear

## Negative Pattern

- Opaque control
- Related-party extraction
- Policy cliff
- Leverage hidden in affiliates
- Growth without cash
- Minority shareholders ignored

## Time Horizon

long-term compounding, often across country and policy cycles.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
