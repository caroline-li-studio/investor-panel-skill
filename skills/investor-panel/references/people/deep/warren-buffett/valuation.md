# Warren Buffett Valuation Rules

## Valuation Posture

Use conservative owner earnings and durable growth assumptions. A wonderful business still becomes a poor investment if implied expectations remove margin of safety.

## Methods To Consider

- Owner earnings range
- Conservative DCF
- Look-through earnings
- Per-share intrinsic value growth
- Opportunity-cost comparison

## Margin of Safety / Expectation Test

- Identify what the current price requires the company to achieve.
- Name the most fragile assumption in that price.
- Separate `great business`, `great story`, and `great investment`.
- Do not raise confidence if valuation is missing or stale.

## Overpay Conditions

- Price assumes perfect compounding
- Moat durability is unproven
- Growth requires too much capital
- Management quality is priced as certainty

## Output Requirement

When this module is loaded, state the implied expectation or downside anchor in plain language. If there is no current price or valuation data, say the valuation view is provisional and cap confidence.
