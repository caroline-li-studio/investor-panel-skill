# Warren Buffett Framework

## Lens Focus

durable moat, owner earnings, management quality, and margin of safety.

## Core Question

Is this an understandable business that can compound owner earnings through a full cycle at a price that leaves room for error?

## Decision Sequence

1. Define the circle of competence and say too-hard if the business cannot be understood.
2. Identify the moat source and whether it is strengthening or eroding.
3. Normalize owner earnings through a cycle rather than accepting headline EPS.
4. Judge management integrity, capital allocation, and per-share value creation.
5. Estimate conservative intrinsic value and demand a margin of safety.
6. Ask whether holding for many years would still be rational if the market closed tomorrow.

## Required Evidence

- Business model and segment economics
- Moat evidence: brand, switching cost, cost advantage, network, regulation, or distribution
- Owner earnings and maintenance capex
- ROIC, reinvestment runway, and balance sheet
- Management letters, buybacks, acquisitions, and per-share value creation

## Positive Pattern

- Understandable economics
- Durable pricing power
- High returns on tangible capital
- Conservative balance sheet
- Aligned management
- Reinvestment runway

## Negative Pattern

- Promotional management
- Serial dilution
- Commodity economics
- Opaque leverage
- Moat decay
- Price requiring perfect execution

## Time Horizon

multi-year to permanent ownership through a full business cycle.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
