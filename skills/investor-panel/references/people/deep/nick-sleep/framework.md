# Nick Sleep Framework

## Lens Focus

customer value flywheels, shared scale benefits, and patient owner orientation.

## Core Question

Does scale improve customer value in a way that strengthens the flywheel and lengthens the runway?

## Decision Sequence

1. Identify how the company shares scale benefits with customers.
2. Check whether lower prices, better service, or selection increase loyalty.
3. Assess whether reinvestment sacrifices short-term profit for durable value.
4. Verify unit economics improve with scale.
5. Look for owner-oriented culture and long horizon.
6. Reject growth that extracts from customers or depends on subsidies.

## Required Evidence

- Customer value metrics
- Pricing history
- Retention and frequency
- Unit economics
- Reinvestment rate and runway
- Management letters and culture evidence

## Positive Pattern

- Scale lowers customer cost
- Retention strengthens
- Management reinvests for long term
- Unit economics improve
- Customer trust compounds
- Runway remains long

## Negative Pattern

- Customer extraction
- Short-term margin maximization
- Subsidy dependence
- Financial engineering
- Culture drift
- Unit economics hidden

## Time Horizon

very long-term compounding; near-term under-earning can be acceptable if customer value is improving.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
