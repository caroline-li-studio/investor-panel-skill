# David Swensen Source Map

Use this source map to ground the David Swensen-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Pioneering Portfolio Management
- Yale endowment reports
- Public talks and investment office materials

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Yale model allocations
- Illiquidity premium examples
- Manager selection discipline
- Alternative assets governance
- Rebalancing through cycles

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Chasing recent returns
- Illiquidity without premium
- Fee drag and weak governance

## Too-Hard Cases

- Portfolio context absent for allocation decisions
- Liquidity needs unknown
- Manager incentives or governance undisclosed

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `David Swensen-style lens` or `institutional portfolio stewardship lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
