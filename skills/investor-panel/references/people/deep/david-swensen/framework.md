# David Swensen Framework

## Lens Focus

portfolio role, governance, liquidity, asset allocation, and long-horizon discipline.

## Core Question

What role does this asset play in a resilient portfolio, and are governance, fees, and liquidity risks compensated?

## Decision Sequence

1. Define the portfolio role: return driver, diversifier, liquidity reserve, inflation hedge, or opportunistic bet.
2. Assess governance, stewardship, and agency costs.
3. Measure liquidity needs and whether illiquidity is compensated.
4. Compare expected return to simpler public alternatives.
5. Check rebalancing value and correlation in stress.
6. Reject return-chasing without structural edge.

## Required Evidence

- Portfolio objective and time horizon
- Liquidity needs and drawdown tolerance
- Governance and fee structure
- Correlation and factor exposures
- Expected return and rebalancing policy
- Manager or management incentives

## Positive Pattern

- Clear portfolio role
- Aligned governance
- Compensated illiquidity
- Long horizon match
- Low agency cost
- Diversifying return stream

## Negative Pattern

- Recent performance chasing
- Opaque fees
- Liquidity mismatch
- Governance weakness
- No diversification benefit
- Complexity without edge

## Time Horizon

institutional multi-cycle horizon; liquidity and governance determine suitability.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
