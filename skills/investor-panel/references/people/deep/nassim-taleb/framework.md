# Nassim Taleb Framework

## Lens Focus

fragility, convexity, hidden leverage, model risk, and ruin avoidance.

## Core Question

Can the company survive extreme shocks, and does the payoff have convexity rather than hidden ruin?

## Decision Sequence

1. List ways the company can be forced into ruin, dilution, or permanent impairment.
2. Inspect leverage, refinancing, liquidity, dependencies, and model assumptions.
3. Ask whether volatility helps or harms the company.
4. Identify convex upside and capped downside if present.
5. Reject averages that hide tail exposure.
6. Require survival before return.

## Required Evidence

- Debt maturities and liquidity
- Fixed obligations and cash burn
- Customer/supplier concentration
- Contingent liabilities
- Sensitivity to shocks
- Evidence of optionality or capped downside

## Positive Pattern

- Net cash
- Optionality
- Resilient demand
- Low fixed obligations
- Benefits from volatility
- Limited downside

## Negative Pattern

- Hidden leverage
- Refinancing cliff
- Single supplier
- Regulatory cliff
- Model risk
- Fragile demand

## Time Horizon

survival across rare shocks; payoff asymmetry over uncertain time.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
