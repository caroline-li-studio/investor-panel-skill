# Nassim Taleb Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Nassim Taleb-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Incerto books
- Public lectures and papers on fragility and convexity
- Risk-management discussions around fat tails and model error

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Option-like convex exposures
- Leverage-induced ruin examples
- Barbell risk posture
- Fragile financial institutions
- Antifragile cash-rich optionality

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Expected value that includes ruin
- Leveraged business dependent on stable refinancing
- Model-based certainty in fat-tailed environments

## Too-Hard Cases

- Risks with unobservable tail exposure
- Opaque derivatives or guarantees
- Supply chains with unknown single points of failure

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Nassim Taleb-style lens` or `tail risk and convexity lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
