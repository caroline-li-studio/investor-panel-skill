# Cathie Wood Valuation Rules

## Valuation Posture

Use long-horizon scenario valuation. Upside must come from explicit adoption, margin, and platform assumptions, not a generic TAM slide.

## Methods To Consider

- Scenario DCF
- Adoption S-curve model
- TAM share sensitivity
- Unit economics at maturity
- Dilution-adjusted value

## Margin of Safety / Expectation Test

- Identify what the current price requires the company to achieve.
- Name the most fragile assumption in that price.
- Separate `great business`, `great story`, and `great investment`.
- Do not raise confidence if valuation is missing or stale.

## Overpay Conditions

- All optionality is priced as certainty
- TAM is counted without margin economics
- Dilution is ignored
- Terminal share is implausible

## Output Requirement

When this module is loaded, state the implied expectation or downside anchor in plain language. If there is no current price or valuation data, say the valuation view is provisional and cap confidence.
