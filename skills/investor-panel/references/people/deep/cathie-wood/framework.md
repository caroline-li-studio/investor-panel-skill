# Cathie Wood Framework

## Lens Focus

disruptive platforms, cost curves, S-curves, optionality, and TAM expansion.

## Core Question

Is this company on the right side of a major cost curve, adoption curve, or platform shift with upside conventional models miss?

## Decision Sequence

1. Identify the technology platform and what cost/performance curve is changing.
2. Estimate adoption S-curve, TAM expansion, and winner-take-most dynamics.
3. Check whether the company has data, network, distribution, or integration advantage.
4. Translate optionality into scenario probabilities rather than slogans.
5. Test cash runway, dilution, and competitive commoditization.
6. Downgrade if innovation evidence is narrative-only.

## Required Evidence

- Unit cost curves and performance metrics
- Adoption rates and customer cohorts
- TAM methodology
- R&D roadmap and product milestones
- Gross margin potential at scale
- Cash runway and dilution

## Positive Pattern

- Falling cost curve
- Large expanding TAM
- Network or data advantage
- Platform optionality
- Rapid adoption evidence
- Economics improve with scale

## Negative Pattern

- Buzzword exposure only
- High cash burn with weak milestones
- Competition commoditizes technology
- Dilution funds the dream
- TAM double counts markets
- Adoption stalls

## Time Horizon

five-year-plus innovation cycle, with milestone-based confidence updates.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
