# Benjamin Graham Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Benjamin Graham-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Security Analysis
- The Intelligent Investor
- Graham-Newman case histories and defensive investor screens

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Net-net portfolios
- Northern Pipeline asset case
- GEICO as a quality exception
- Defensive investor screens
- Cigar-butt asset bargains

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- High-multiple growth stocks without asset protection
- Companies with unstable earnings and heavy debt
- Story stocks priced far above tangible evidence

## Too-Hard Cases

- Financials with opaque assets
- Early-stage firms with no earnings record
- Businesses where liquidation value is unknowable

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Benjamin Graham-style lens` or `defensive value lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
