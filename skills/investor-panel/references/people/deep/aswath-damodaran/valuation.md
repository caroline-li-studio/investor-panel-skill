# Aswath Damodaran Valuation Rules

## Valuation Posture

Use DCF, reverse DCF, scenario ranges, and implied-expectations tests. The point is not a precise target price; it is exposing what must be true.

## Methods To Consider

- Reverse DCF
- Bear/base/bull DCF
- Unit economics to terminal margin bridge
- Peer sanity check after adjusting for growth and reinvestment

## Margin of Safety / Expectation Test

- Identify what the current price requires the company to achieve.
- Name the most fragile assumption in that price.
- Separate `great business`, `great story`, and `great investment`.
- Do not raise confidence if valuation is missing or stale.

## Overpay Conditions

- The price requires dominant share, elite margins, low reinvestment, and low risk at once
- Terminal value supplies nearly all upside
- A small WACC or margin change erases the thesis

## Output Requirement

When this module is loaded, state the implied expectation or downside anchor in plain language. If there is no current price or valuation data, say the valuation view is provisional and cap confidence.
