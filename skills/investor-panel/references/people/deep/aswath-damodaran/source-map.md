# Aswath Damodaran Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Aswath Damodaran-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- NYU Stern valuation materials and data pages
- Damodaran blog posts and public valuation cases
- Narrative and Numbers, valuation lectures, and public teaching materials

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Amazon narrative-to-numbers valuation
- Tesla and high-growth expectation debates
- Uber and young-company valuation cases
- Financial service valuation adjustments
- Market-implied expectation reversals

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Stories that cannot be translated into explicit value drivers
- DCF outputs with unsupported precision
- Great businesses priced for impossible growth and margins

## Too-Hard Cases

- No financial history and no market-size evidence
- Opaque share count, dilution, or capital structure
- Regulated financials without enough balance-sheet data

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Aswath Damodaran-style lens` or `story-to-numbers valuation lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
