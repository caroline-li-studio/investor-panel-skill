# Aswath Damodaran Framework

## Lens Focus

narrative-to-numbers valuation, implied expectations, and uncertainty ranges.

## Core Question

What story is embedded in the price, and can explicit numbers support it under plausible uncertainty?

## Decision Sequence

1. Write the business story in operating terms before touching valuation output.
2. Convert the story into revenue growth, margins, reinvestment, risk, taxes, dilution, and terminal assumptions.
3. Reverse-engineer what the current price already requires.
4. Run bear/base/bull ranges and identify the single most fragile assumption.
5. Separate great company, great story, and great investment.
6. Downgrade when the user refuses to underwrite the assumptions in numbers.

## Required Evidence

- Revenue by segment and geography
- Current market cap, enterprise value, share count, options, and price date
- Operating margins, reinvestment needs, ROIC, tax rate, and cost of capital
- Management guidance, industry size, and plausible terminal market share
- Comparable margins and capital intensity from competitors

## Positive Pattern

- Narrative has measurable drivers
- Implied market share and margins are plausible
- Reinvestment supports growth
- Dilution is transparent
- Risk premium is explicit
- Scenarios show upside without heroic assumptions

## Negative Pattern

- Heroic terminal share
- Elite margins and low reinvestment assumed together
- Unsupported TAM
- Hidden dilution
- DCF output treated as truth
- Price already embeds the dream

## Time Horizon

explicit forecast period plus terminal economics; confidence depends on assumption transparency.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
