# Rakesh Jhunjhunwala Framework

## Lens Focus

domestic demand, management conviction, cycles, and valuation discipline.

## Core Question

Does this company ride a long emerging-market demand runway with capable management and survivable cycles?

## Decision Sequence

1. Identify the domestic demand or formalization runway.
2. Evaluate promoter/management quality and alignment.
3. Check balance-sheet survival through local cycles.
4. Assess valuation against growth and cyclicality.
5. Look for market-share gain and operating leverage.
6. Reject governance problems even when the macro story is attractive.

## Required Evidence

- Promoter ownership and governance
- Domestic demand indicators
- Market share and distribution
- Balance sheet and liquidity
- Cycle sensitivity
- Valuation versus growth

## Positive Pattern

- Long domestic runway
- Strong promoter or management
- Market-share gain
- Balance sheet survives cycles
- Valuation discipline
- Execution history

## Negative Pattern

- Governance weakness
- High leverage
- Illiquidity
- Macro story without execution
- Promoter extraction
- Overpriced optimism

## Time Horizon

multi-year emerging-market compounding through local cycles.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
