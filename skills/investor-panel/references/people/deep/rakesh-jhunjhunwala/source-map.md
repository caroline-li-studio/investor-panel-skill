# Rakesh Jhunjhunwala Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Rakesh Jhunjhunwala-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Public interviews and talks
- Public portfolio case studies
- Indian market compounding and cycle examples

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Titan-like consumer compounding
- Financial formalization winners
- Domestic demand leaders
- Cyclical upturns with strong balance sheets
- Promoter-led scale stories

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Governance red flags
- Leverage in weak cycle
- Illiquid hype stocks

## Too-Hard Cases

- Disclosure too weak
- Promoter incentives unclear
- Macro thesis without company execution

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Rakesh Jhunjhunwala-style lens` or `emerging-market growth value lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
