# Julian Robertson Framework

## Lens Focus

management quality, relative winners and losers, and disciplined long/short comparison.

## Core Question

Which companies are structurally winning or losing within the same ecosystem, and is management quality aligned with that view?

## Decision Sequence

1. Define the industry ecosystem and peer set.
2. Identify structural winners and losers using management, share, margins, and capital allocation.
3. Compare valuation to relative quality and growth.
4. For shorts, demand structural weakness and timing/catalyst awareness.
5. Control crowding and borrow risk.
6. Prefer paired relative insight over isolated stories.

## Required Evidence

- Peer share and margin trends
- Management track record
- Earnings revisions
- Valuation spreads
- Short interest and borrow conditions
- Competitive positioning

## Positive Pattern

- Superior management
- Share gain
- Improving revisions
- Durable competitive edge
- Reasonable valuation versus peers
- Clear loser on other side

## Negative Pattern

- Weak management
- Share loss
- Crowded long
- No short catalyst
- Valuation ignores execution risk
- Industry data missing

## Time Horizon

multi-quarter to multi-year fundamental trend, with market discipline.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
