# Julian Robertson Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Julian Robertson-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Tiger Management histories and interviews
- Public Robertson letters and comments
- Tiger-style long/short case studies

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Tiger-style quality longs
- Structural shorts in weak competitors
- Management-led share gain
- Sector pair trades
- Global growth franchises

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Good story with bad management
- Crowded winner without improving revisions
- Short without structural weakness

## Too-Hard Cases

- Peer data missing
- Management quality unclear
- No borrow or catalyst for short side

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Julian Robertson-style lens` or `Tiger long/short fundamentals lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
