# Mohnish Pabrai Framework

## Lens Focus

low-risk high-uncertainty bets, cloning, owner-operators, and downside-first odds.

## Core Question

Is this a simple bet where downside is limited, uncertainty scares others, and upside is meaningfully asymmetric?

## Decision Sequence

1. Explain the business simply and reject complexity.
2. Separate uncertainty from risk of permanent loss.
3. Estimate downside first and ask whether it is limited.
4. Look for proven patterns or cloned ideas from excellent investors.
5. Check owner-operator incentives and balance-sheet survival.
6. Buy only when odds are asymmetric and price compensates uncertainty.

## Required Evidence

- Simple business model and cash generation
- Downside asset or earnings support
- Balance sheet survival
- Owner/operator incentives
- Comparable proven playbooks
- Valuation versus normalized earnings or assets

## Positive Pattern

- Simple business
- Limited downside
- High uncertainty but low permanent loss risk
- Owner-operator
- Cloned proven idea
- Large upside if normalizes

## Negative Pattern

- Complexity
- Leverage can kill
- Downside not bounded
- No owner alignment
- Price does not compensate uncertainty
- Permanent impairment risk

## Time Horizon

multi-year normalization or compounding, with patience because uncertainty may last.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
