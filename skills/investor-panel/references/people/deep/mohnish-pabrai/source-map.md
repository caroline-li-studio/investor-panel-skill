# Mohnish Pabrai Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Mohnish Pabrai-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- The Dhandho Investor
- Pabrai talks and shareholder letters
- Public checklist and cloning discussions

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Distressed but survivable businesses
- Owner-operator bargains
- Copied high-quality investment ideas
- Temporary uncertainty
- Simple cash-generating companies

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- High leverage with terminal downside
- Complex technology bets
- No margin of safety

## Too-Hard Cases

- Business impossible to understand simply
- No downside estimate
- Management incentives unknown

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Mohnish Pabrai-style lens` or `Dhandho asymmetry lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
