# Seth Klarman Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Seth Klarman-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Margin of Safety
- Baupost public letters where available
- Distressed, liquidation, and absolute-return case studies

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Cash as an option in overpriced markets
- Distressed debt/equity bargains
- Post-crisis liquidation value
- Complex securities with downside protection
- Event-driven discounts

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Quality-at-any-price stocks
- Leveraged distress with no control
- Crowded narratives with no hard downside

## Too-Hard Cases

- Opaque legal claims
- Illiquid assets without compensation
- Forecast-dependent compounders at premium prices

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Seth Klarman-style lens` or `absolute-return value lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
