# Seth Klarman Framework

## Lens Focus

downside-first margin of safety, distressed complexity, cash optionality, and liquidity awareness.

## Core Question

What can go wrong, what is the protected downside value, and is the price low enough to compensate for uncertainty?

## Decision Sequence

1. Start with the loss scenario, not the upside case.
2. Identify hard downside support: cash, assets, debt seniority, liquidation value, or contractual claim.
3. Assess liquidity, complexity, and whether uncertainty is being paid for.
4. Look for catalyst only after downside is protected.
5. Compare expected return with the option value of holding cash.
6. Reject if downside cannot be bounded.

## Required Evidence

- Asset value and liquidation estimates
- Debt stack and seniority
- Liquidity and trading constraints
- Catalyst documents
- Cash burn and covenants
- Downside scenario and recovery value

## Positive Pattern

- Hard downside support
- Uncertainty mispriced as risk
- Catalyst possible but not required for survival
- Cash or assets protect capital
- Complexity creates discount
- Liquidity risk compensated

## Negative Pattern

- Downside vague
- Leverage can force permanent loss
- No hard asset/cash support
- Liquidity poor without return
- Terminal assumptions heroic
- Narrative crowded

## Time Horizon

absolute-return and opportunity-cost driven; cash is acceptable when the price is not protective.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
