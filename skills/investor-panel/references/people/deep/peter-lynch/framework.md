# Peter Lynch Framework

## Lens Focus

plain-language business classification, visible growth, and valuation sanity.

## Core Question

Can the business story be explained simply, classified correctly, and bought at a valuation that does not outrun growth?

## Decision Sequence

1. Explain the business in one plain paragraph.
2. Classify it as slow grower, stalwart, fast grower, cyclical, turnaround, or asset play.
3. Identify observable customer, store, product, or unit evidence.
4. Check earnings, cash generation, balance sheet, and PEG-style valuation sanity.
5. Ask what will change the category or end the story.
6. Reject exciting stories with no earnings evidence.

## Required Evidence

- Simple business model
- Company category and growth drivers
- Unit/store/customer/product evidence
- Earnings growth and cash generation
- Balance sheet and debt
- Valuation versus growth

## Positive Pattern

- Simple product
- Visible customer traction
- Same-store or unit economics
- Reasonable growth valuation
- Low debt
- Insider ownership

## Negative Pattern

- Story too popular
- Earnings lag story
- Category misclassified
- Debt in turnarounds
- Growth already priced in
- Diworsification

## Time Horizon

multi-year business story, adjusted by company category.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
