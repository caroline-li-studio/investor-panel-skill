# Peter Lynch Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Peter Lynch-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- One Up on Wall Street
- Beating the Street
- Fidelity Magellan interviews and case discussions

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Dunkin' Donuts
- Taco Bell
- La Quinta
- Chrysler turnaround
- Retail and local-observation field research

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Hot concept stocks with no earnings path
- Diworsification stories
- Growth names already priced for perfection

## Too-Hard Cases

- Complex technology without observable customer economics
- Financial engineering stories
- Businesses that cannot be explained simply

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Peter Lynch-style lens` or `understandable growth lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
