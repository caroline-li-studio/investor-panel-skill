# Peter Lynch Valuation Rules

## Valuation Posture

Valuation must fit the category. Growth at any price is rejected; slow growers need value/yield, fast growers need durable earnings growth.

## Methods To Consider

- PEG sanity check
- Category-specific multiple
- Turnaround survival value
- Asset play value
- Earnings growth versus P/E

## Margin of Safety / Expectation Test

- Identify what the current price requires the company to achieve.
- Name the most fragile assumption in that price.
- Separate `great business`, `great story`, and `great investment`.
- Do not raise confidence if valuation is missing or stale.

## Overpay Conditions

- Growth story is obvious and fully priced
- P/E outruns earnings growth
- Turnaround priced before survival evidence

## Output Requirement

When this module is loaded, state the implied expectation or downside anchor in plain language. If there is no current price or valuation data, say the valuation view is provisional and cap confidence.
