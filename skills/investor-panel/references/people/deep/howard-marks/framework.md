# Howard Marks Framework

## Lens Focus

second-level thinking, cycle position, credit risk, and offense/defense balance.

## Core Question

What does consensus believe, where are we in the cycle, and are we paid enough for the risk?

## Decision Sequence

1. State the first-level consensus view.
2. Ask the second-level question: what is wrong or already priced?
3. Locate the market and credit cycle.
4. Distinguish volatility from permanent loss risk.
5. Choose offense or defense based on risk compensation.
6. Reject if prospective return does not compensate adverse scenarios.

## Required Evidence

- Consensus expectations
- Credit spreads and lending conditions
- Valuation relative to cycle history
- Leverage and covenant quality
- Liquidity conditions
- Downside scenario and recovery

## Positive Pattern

- Pessimism priced in
- Risk aversion creates compensation
- Strong covenants or assets
- Cycle closer to trough
- Liquidity premium is real
- Downside understood

## Negative Pattern

- Euphoria
- Easy money extrapolated
- Risk premiums thin
- Leverage ignored
- Consensus bullish
- No margin for adverse cycle

## Time Horizon

cycle-aware; offense or defense changes with risk compensation.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
