# Howard Marks Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Howard Marks-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Oaktree memos
- The Most Important Thing
- Mastering the Market Cycle

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Distressed debt after panic
- Credit cycle bargains
- Overheated market warnings
- Illiquidity premiums
- Defensive shifts in euphoric markets

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- First-level growth extrapolation
- Low spread for high risk
- Optimism at cycle peak

## Too-Hard Cases

- Cycle position unknowable
- Debt documents missing
- Consensus view not identified

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Howard Marks-style lens` or `cycle and risk discipline lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
