# John Templeton Source Map

Use this source map to ground the John Templeton-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Templeton writings and interviews
- Templeton Growth Fund history
- Contrarian global investing case studies

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Post-war global bargains
- Country-level pessimism opportunities
- Out-of-favor industries
- Currency-driven pessimism
- Survivable unpopular companies

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Cheap because governance is broken
- Permanent structural decline
- Capital controls with no shareholder protection

## Too-Hard Cases

- No reliable shareholder rights
- Political risk cannot be bounded
- Financial data not comparable across markets

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `John Templeton-style lens` or `global contrarian value lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
