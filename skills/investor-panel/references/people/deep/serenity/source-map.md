# Serenity Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Serenity-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Public Serenity-style supply-chain research patterns
- Company filings, transcripts, patents, standards, and project documents
- Customer, supplier, and trade-source cross-checks

## Canonical Cases To Study

- AI data-center power bottlenecks
- Semiconductor materials qualification
- Battery supply-chain constraints
- Industrial automation scarce components
- High-purity specialty inputs

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Theme-adjacent company without bottleneck control
- Promotional evidence only
- Capacity that can expand quickly

## Too-Hard Cases

- Private supply chains with no public verification
- Claims with no customer certification evidence
- Bottlenecks that migrate too quickly to map

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Serenity-style lens` or `supply-chain research lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
