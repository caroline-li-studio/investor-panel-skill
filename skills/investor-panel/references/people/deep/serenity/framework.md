# Serenity Framework

## Lens Focus

market story to system change to scarce value-chain layer to evidence.

## Core Question

Where is the scarce, hard-to-scale layer in the value chain, and does this company control it with verifiable evidence?

## Decision Sequence

1. Translate the market story into a concrete system change.
2. Map the full value chain before naming winners.
3. Rank layers by supplier count, qualification time, capacity, IP, regulation, and certification.
4. Collect primary and cross-checked public evidence.
5. Separate bottleneck owners from theme passengers.
6. Pass finalists to valuation and risk lenses.

## Required Evidence

- Value-chain map
- Supplier and customer relationships
- Qualification cycles and certifications
- Capacity data and lead times
- Patents, standards, regulatory/project documents
- Contracts, backlog, and pricing evidence

## Positive Pattern

- Low supplier count
- Long qualification
- Hard capacity expansion
- Customer certification
- Pricing power
- Primary evidence

## Negative Pattern

- Theme label only
- Quick capacity expansion
- Weak evidence
- Low bargaining power
- Substitution risk
- Bottleneck migration

## Time Horizon

theme cycle to supply-chain capacity cycle.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
