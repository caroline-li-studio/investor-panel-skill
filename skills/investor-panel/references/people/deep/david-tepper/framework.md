# David Tepper Framework

## Lens Focus

capital structure survival, panic pricing, and policy turns.

## Core Question

Can the capital structure survive until policy, liquidity, or normalization unlocks large upside?

## Decision Sequence

1. Map the capital structure before judging the equity.
2. Identify panic pricing and the source of forced selling.
3. Check maturities, covenants, liquidity, and recovery values.
4. Assess whether policy, credit, or liquidity conditions are turning.
5. Estimate downside through the debt stack and upside through normalization.
6. Reject if survival depends on hope or creditor generosity.

## Required Evidence

- Debt stack, maturities, covenants, and interest cost
- Liquidity and refinancing options
- Recovery value and asset coverage
- Policy or credit-market signals
- Equity dilution risk
- Scenario EBITDA/FCF under stress

## Positive Pattern

- Panic pricing
- Survivable balance sheet
- Policy or liquidity turn
- Assets cover downside
- Normalization upside
- Market extrapolates temporary stress

## Negative Pattern

- Bankruptcy likely
- Creditors control outcome
- Dilution unavoidable
- Policy already priced
- No liquidity bridge
- Assets impaired

## Time Horizon

distress-to-normalization cycle; timing depends on liquidity and capital-structure survival.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
