# David Tepper Source Map

Use this source map to ground the David Tepper-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Appaloosa public interviews
- Distressed credit and equity case histories
- Crisis investing discussions around policy and liquidity turns

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Post-crisis bank recovery
- Distressed credit rebounds
- Policy-supported assets
- Panic pricing in cyclicals
- Capital-structure mispricing

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Equity below impaired debt
- Refinancing cliff with no bridge
- Policy support fully priced in

## Too-Hard Cases

- Debt stack unavailable
- Recovery value unknowable
- Legal control sits with creditors and equity has no path

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `David Tepper-style lens` or `distressed macro value lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
