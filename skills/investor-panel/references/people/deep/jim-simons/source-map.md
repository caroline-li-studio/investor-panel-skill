# Jim Simons Source Map

Use this source map to ground the Jim Simons-style lens. Treat it as a research checklist, not as proof that the real investor would hold the same current opinion.

## Primary Source Targets

- Public interviews with Jim Simons and Renaissance principals
- The Man Who Solved the Market
- Quantitative finance research principles on data leakage, costs, and robustness

## Canonical Cases To Study

- Short-term statistical anomalies
- Signal decay examples
- Market microstructure effects
- Alternative data tests
- Capacity-constrained strategies

## Cases This Lens Should Resist

- Anecdote as evidence
- Overfit backtest
- Crowded signal without capacity analysis

## Too-Hard Cases

- No dataset
- No out-of-sample test
- Costs or capacity unknown

## Avoided Sources

- Unsourced quote collections and motivational summaries.
- Social-media claims about current views unless backed by primary records.
- Single anecdotes that do not recur across the investor's broader method.
- Long copyrighted passages. Use short references and paraphrase the framework.

## Citation Rules

- Prefer source-type citations such as shareholder letters, fund memos, lectures, filings, books, course material, or case documents.
- Use `Jim Simons-style lens` or `statistical evidence lens`; do not imply the real investor currently agrees.
- If primary evidence is not available in the evidence packet, lower confidence and name the missing source.
