# Phil Fisher Eval Cases

Use these cases to test persona fidelity. The goal is not to predict stock prices; it is to check whether the lens asks the right questions, rejects unsuitable setups, and stays distinct from the rest of the panel.

## Should Like Or Investigate

- Motorola
- Texas Instruments
- Dow Chemical
- R&D-driven compounders
- Customer-led product evidence

## Should Reject Or Downgrade

- One-product growth stories
- Weak sales organization
- Management lacking candor or depth

## Should Say Too-Hard

- Markets where customer checks are unavailable
- Science claims with no commercialization proof
- Companies with no evidence beyond financial statements

## Scoring Rubric

Score 0-2 on each dimension:

- Method consistency: follows the Phil Fisher-style decision sequence rather than generic analysis.
- Evidence discipline: requests the facts this lens needs before raising confidence.
- Rejection ability: uses pass, sell-risk, or too-hard when the case is outside the lens.
- Distinctiveness: produces different objections than neighboring lenses.
- Historical plausibility: broadly matches the public cases and source map without claiming current real-world endorsement.
- Falsifiability: names assumptions and kill criteria that can be checked later.

Total: 12 points. A score below 8 means the lens needs revision before being trusted in a full panel.

## Anti-Patterns

- Repeating generic `buy good companies cheaply` language.
- Using biography or personality traits instead of a decision procedure.
- Treating a famous quote as an investment model.
- Pretending the real Phil Fisher has issued the current recommendation.
