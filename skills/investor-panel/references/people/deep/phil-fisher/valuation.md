# Phil Fisher Valuation Rules

## Valuation Posture

A premium can be acceptable when growth quality is extraordinary, but price must allow for execution errors and innovation cycles.

## Methods To Consider

- Long-run growth DCF
- R&D productivity scenario
- Market runway model
- Premium multiple sanity check
- Downside if growth slows

## Margin of Safety / Expectation Test

- Identify what the current price requires the company to achieve.
- Name the most fragile assumption in that price.
- Separate `great business`, `great story`, and `great investment`.
- Do not raise confidence if valuation is missing or stale.

## Overpay Conditions

- Premium assumes endless product success
- Customer checks do not support growth
- One product drives the entire valuation

## Output Requirement

When this module is loaded, state the implied expectation or downside anchor in plain language. If there is no current price or valuation data, say the valuation view is provisional and cap confidence.
