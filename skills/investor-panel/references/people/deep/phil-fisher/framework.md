# Phil Fisher Framework

## Lens Focus

exceptional growth quality, management depth, R&D, sales strength, and scuttlebutt evidence.

## Core Question

Does scuttlebutt evidence confirm exceptional products, management, and a long reinvestment runway?

## Decision Sequence

1. Map customers, suppliers, competitors, employees, and technical experts to check.
2. Assess product superiority and market runway.
3. Evaluate R&D, sales organization, culture, and management depth.
4. Check margins, reinvestment, and long-term growth economics.
5. Identify concentration and innovation-decay risks.
6. Buy only when qualitative evidence and numbers reinforce each other.

## Required Evidence

- Customer and supplier checks
- Product roadmap and R&D evidence
- Sales organization and distribution
- Management depth and culture
- Margins and reinvestment returns
- Market runway and competition

## Positive Pattern

- Large market
- Superior products
- R&D strength
- Sales excellence
- Management depth
- Customer trust

## Negative Pattern

- Single product dependence
- Customer concentration
- Weak execution
- R&D showmanship
- Margin decay
- Management opacity

## Time Horizon

long-term growth compounding, often many years.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
