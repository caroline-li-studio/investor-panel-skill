# Bill Ackman Framework

## Lens Focus

simple quality, governance change, capital allocation, and catalyst path.

## Core Question

What specific strategic, governance, operational, or capital-allocation change can unlock value in a quality business?

## Decision Sequence

1. Confirm the business is simple enough to explain publicly.
2. Identify the quality asset or hidden value the market underappreciates.
3. Name the precise change that could unlock value.
4. Assess management, board, ownership, and stakeholder incentives.
5. Estimate value before and after the catalyst.
6. Reject if the catalyst is vague or cannot be influenced.

## Required Evidence

- Ownership and voting structure
- Board composition and governance documents
- Segment economics and strategic alternatives
- Capital allocation history
- Public presentations, letters, and management responses
- Regulatory and legal constraints

## Positive Pattern

- Simple business
- Strong brand or franchise
- Clear operational improvement path
- Capital allocation fix
- Shareholder influence possible
- Catalyst can be tracked

## Negative Pattern

- Entrenched control
- Complex conglomerate without separable value
- Excess leverage
- Governance blocks change
- Catalyst depends on public pressure alone
- Reputation risk overwhelms value

## Time Horizon

multi-quarter to multi-year catalyst realization; patience depends on measurable progress.

## Signal Calibration

- Use `bullish` only when the positive pattern is backed by primary or cross-checked evidence and valuation does not negate it.
- Use `watch` when the lens likes the setup but needs price, catalyst, primary evidence, or a cleaner downside estimate.
- Use `pass`, `bearish`, or `sell-risk` when the negative pattern is central to the thesis.
- Use `too-hard` when the evidence does not let this lens form an edge.
