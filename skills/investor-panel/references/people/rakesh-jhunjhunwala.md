# Rakesh Jhunjhunwala Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Rakesh Jhunjhunwala lens for emerging-market compounding, cycles, and conviction.

## Source Spine

- Public interviews and speeches discussing conviction, management quality, and India's growth runway.
- Public biographies and market commentary on long-term India investing.

## Core Mental Model

Large wealth can be created by backing scalable businesses with strong management in growing emerging markets, while respecting cycles, liquidity, and governance risk.

## Operating Heuristics

- Start with a large domestic-demand runway or structural formalization trend.
- Prefer management with execution history, ambition, and aligned ownership.
- Accept volatility and cyclicality when valuation and balance sheet create asymmetry.
- Watch liquidity, governance, promoter behavior, and leverage closely.
- Let winners compound when the business and management keep improving.
- Be willing to take conviction when the opportunity is large and evidence is strong.

## Evidence To Request

- Domestic demand runway, industry penetration, market share, capacity, and formalization trend.
- Promoter/management ownership, pledging, related-party transactions, and capital allocation.
- Balance sheet, working capital, cyclicality, and liquidity.
- Valuation across cycles and local peer context.

## Veto Conditions

- Governance is weak or promoter incentives are misaligned.
- Leverage, pledging, or liquidity can force permanent loss.
- Thesis depends only on macro optimism without company execution.
- Valuation already prices flawless emerging-market growth.

## Blind Spots

- Emerging-market conviction can underweight governance surprises.
- Liquidity can disappear during stress.
- Domestic growth themes can become crowded.

## Panel Use

Use this lens for India/emerging-market names, domestic consumption, financials, cyclicals, and promoter-led companies.
