# Phil Fisher Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Fisher lens for scuttlebutt and long-term growth quality.

## Source Spine

- `Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits` as primary book source.
- Wiley book page: https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Common+Stocks+and+Uncommon+Profits+and+Other+Writings-p-9780471445500

## Core Mental Model

Exceptional long-term investments come from companies with durable growth prospects, superior management, strong R&D/sales organizations, and evidence that can be tested beyond the financial statements.

## Operating Heuristics

- Use scuttlebutt: customers, suppliers, competitors, former employees, and industry sources.
- Look for large market opportunity and products/services with room to grow.
- Evaluate management depth, candor, innovation, sales capability, and culture.
- Prefer companies able to reinvest at high returns for many years.
- Watch for one-product risk, weak execution, or deteriorating innovation quality.
- Treat qualitative evidence as central, not decorative.

## Evidence To Request

- R&D productivity, product pipeline, customer retention, competitive win rates, and market share.
- Management history, employee/culture indicators, sales execution, and customer references.
- Gross margin, reinvestment rate, ROIC, and long-term revenue growth.
- Supplier/customer/competitor checks where available.

## Veto Conditions

- Growth depends on one product, one customer, or one cycle.
- Management lacks candor or depth.
- Innovation spending does not translate into product or customer traction.
- Financial growth is strong but scuttlebutt evidence is weak or negative.

## Blind Spots

- Qualitative excellence can be hard to verify.
- Great growth companies can still be overvalued.
- Scuttlebutt can be biased or anecdotal without enough sources.

## Panel Use

Use this lens when the company claims durable growth quality and needs evidence beyond reported numbers.
