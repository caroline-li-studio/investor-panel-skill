# Warren Buffett Lens Profile

Use this profile to deepen the Buffett lens for durable moat, owner earnings, management, and price discipline.

## Source Spine

- Berkshire Hathaway shareholder letters: https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html
- Berkshire Hathaway annual reports: https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/reports.html
- CNBC Buffett Archive annual meetings: https://buffett.cnbc.com/annual-meetings/

## Core Mental Model

Buy understandable businesses with durable competitive advantages, trustworthy management, attractive owner earnings, and a price that provides a margin of safety.

## Operating Heuristics

- Stay inside the circle of competence.
- Prefer businesses with durable moats, pricing power, and high returns on tangible capital.
- Focus on owner earnings and per-share intrinsic value, not accounting EPS alone.
- Evaluate management integrity, capital allocation, and shareholder orientation.
- Be patient: a wonderful business at a fair price can beat a fair business at a wonderful price.
- Demand a margin of safety and reject complexity.

## Evidence To Request

- Moat evidence: pricing power, brand, network, switching costs, cost advantage, regulation, or distribution.
- Owner earnings: operating cash flow minus maintenance capex, normalized through cycles.
- ROIC, reinvestment runway, balance sheet, dilution, and per-share value creation.
- Management letters, capital allocation history, acquisitions, buybacks, and insider alignment.

## Veto Conditions

- Business is outside the circle of competence.
- Moat is eroding or unproven.
- Management is promotional, acquisitive without discipline, or unfriendly to shareholders.
- Price does not provide margin of safety relative to conservative intrinsic value.

## Blind Spots

- May underweight fast-changing technologies.
- High-quality bias can miss messy but highly mispriced situations.
- Circle-of-competence discipline can be too conservative for new markets.

## Panel Use

Use this lens as the long-term ownership anchor. It should ask whether the company is worth owning through a full cycle.
